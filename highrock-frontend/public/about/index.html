High Rock Capital Management Inc. is a portfolio management company registered with the Ontario Securities Commission (OSC), as well as the Securities Commissions of British Columbia, to manage client money on a fully-discretionary basis in those provinces.

High Rock has two distinct yet very synergistic divisions:

  1. Private Client Wealth Management - for Individuals who want professional, total wealth and portfolio management
  2. High Yield and Special Mandates - we manage several high yield bond mandates for corporations, family trusts, endowments and some high net worth individuals

High Rock is a proud member of the Portfolio Managers Association Of Canada (PMAC)

​High Rock is a member in good standing of the Ombudsman for Banking Services and Investments. The OBSI was created to resolve disputes between participating banking services and investment firms and their customers.

Frequently asked questions

What is the difference between discretionary portfolio management and investment advice?

A discretionary portfolio manager has a legal fiduciary duty to the client. An investment advisor only has to ensure suitability of the investment for the client at the point of sale, after which, the client is then liable for the result of the investment. High Rock is a discretionary portfolio manager.

Who regulates High Rock Capital?

High Rock is registered with the Ontario Securities Commission (OSC) and the BC Securities Commission to operate as a discretionary Portfolio Manager with a legal fiduciary duty to our clients at all times. The OSC is not a self-regulating organization (like the banks/dealers who are regulated by IIROC) but a government agency reporting to the Minister of Finance for Ontario.

How do I know my money is safe at High Rock and Raymond James?

High Rock manages money on a Separately Managed Account basis which means it is your portfolio and you always own the securities in it . High Rock cannot take money out of your account as it is held at Raymond James as the custodian, not at High Rock. We never “touch” money. Clients just give us the authority to buy and sell securities in their portfolio. And Raymond James is a member of the Canadian Investor Protection Fund (CIPF) which means that if ever Raymond James becomes insolvent, our client accounts are covered for up to $1mm for each class of accounts: non-registered, registered (RSP, TFSA) and RESP.

What if I want my money back?

It is your money and they are your securities at all times so if you ever want to end our relationship, you simply send an email to one of us (we will follow up with a phone call to ensure the email is legitimate) and state that you want no more trades done in your account and you want to transfer out to a bank or dealer. You are always in control of your account.

What is a Conflict of Interest in the investment industry?

Good examples would be when a mutual fund company pays an advisor to sell its funds (trailer fees) or when Advisors who are paid a commission based on the assets under their administration which puts client’s needs as secondary to their own personal revenue generating activities. High Rock is paid only by our clients.

What is your Voluntary Code of Conduct?

High Rock’s “Voluntary Code of Conduct” is a signed document by all employees that forms the cornerstone of High Rock’s ethos and our promise to put client interests ahead of our own at all times.

What is Fee transparency?

Knowing exactly what % and what $ amount you are / will be paying for investment services and to whom you are paying them. We fully-disclose our fees in an easy to understand way.

Can I deduct the Fees I pay High Rock?

Fees paid to a portfolio manager (for discretionary management) are tax deductible in non-registered (taxable) accounts. Not so for mutual funds. Fees paid to High Rock are inclusive of all services (including custodial costs to RJCS). High Rock revenues cover all expenses including employee salaries. High Rock employees (including the owners) are not paid commissions.

Do you buy Mutual Funds for Clients?

Never in a million years will we own a mutual fund. We do use some Exchange Traded Funds (ETF’s) with extremely low cost fees to gain exposure to global equity markets.

What is a Wealth Forecast and why do I need one?

A Wealth Forecast is created to determine a client’s goals (major purchases, education, retirement, estate), the timelines expected to achieve those goals and the necessary annualized growth target for asset growth required to build the wealth to achieve those goals. The results of the Wealth Forecast form our investment decisions on how much risk a client needs to take to meet their goals. The Wealth Forecast is updated often to keep up with life changes.

Does Raymond James own some or all of High Rock Capital?

No. High Rock is 100% employee-owned so there are no other interests to influence our relationship and service with our clients.

How do you manage risk?

There are many types of risk that we manage (foreign exchange, interest rate, credit, market, etc). The Managing Partners have 30 years each of managing risk on a large-scale basis with balance sheets well into the billions of dollars and we utilize that experience for our clients.

How do you make investment decisions?

Disciplined Investing and based on fundamental research on both a macro and micro basis where we build models of companies to estimate cash flows and other metrics.

How often will you meet with me?

The OSC mandates that we meet with our clients once per year, however, we meet with our clients twice per year. Importantly, we are reachable 24/7 and encourage an open dialogue with our clients. We also write blogs, produce a monthly video, and send out quarterly reports to our clients.

Do employees invest in the same models/securities as clients?

Yes. The bulk of the Managing Partner’s investable assets are in the exact same models and securities as our clients, so our interests are totally aligned. You could say that “we eat our own cooking”.

What is your IRC?

To our knowledge, we are the only Portfolio Management company in the country managing money on a Separately Managed Account basis who has created an Independent Review Committee (IRC) to review the operations of High Rock and report to our clients on a quarterly basis that we are “doing what we said we would do”. Wychcrest Compliance Services reviews our operations, trades, investments etc for the sole benefit of our clients to provide them with a sense of oversight on how we manage our client’s money.

What education do you have to manage people’s money?

We have the highest levels of education in the financial services business. Paul has a CFA (Chartered Financial Analyst) designation. Scott has a Chartered Investment Management (CIM) in Canada. Both Paul and Scott have numerous courses/designations for both education as well as licensing to operate in Canada Dianne has completed the Canadian Securities Course, previously licensed as an IA, she has completed the required continuing education, and also the Chief Compliance officer course.
